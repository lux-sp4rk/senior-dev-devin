# Sarah

Devin's wife. Injured, managing the household alone, knows more than Devin thinks she does.

## Surface Facts

- Leg elevated (car accident — three months recovery, three-car pileup that somehow involved no cars)
- On a couch — the house has reorganized around her limited mobility
- Kids fighting over a broken iPad screen
- Managing the mortgage statement, the insurance fog, the silence in the room

## What She Knows

- The insurance situation is a fog — but she has already processed it in her own head
- The savings account is a rumor — she knows exactly how bad it is
- Devin is drowning before he says anything — she reads him
- The marriage has a different texture than it used to — not broken, but strained under weight

## Voice Moments (from writing)

- "The car was fine." / "The other guy wasn't." — sparse, no emotion, fact-reporting that contains everything
- Delivers hard truths without drama — the quiet is more frightening than shouting

## What She Doesn't Say

- Never asks Devin what happened at work
- Doesn't push on the "three months" of no work — she's already calculated it
- The silence in the room "has a different texture than it used to" — she's noticed, she's catalogued, she hasn't named it

## Character Notes (provisional)

- She is not a victim — she's the most clear-eyed person in the story
- Her restraint is not passivity — it's triage
- The one thing she'd never say out loud: probably something about whether she still believes in Devin

## Open Questions

- Does she know about "The Guy" when Devin creates it?
- What was the first date with Devin like before the pressure?
- What does she do for work / did she do?
