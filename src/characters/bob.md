# Bob (The Former VP)

The "Shield." The man who stood between Devin and the Board for fifteen years. He didn't just know what a pointer was; he could explain the difference between a memory leak and a bad abstraction without looking at a dashboard.

## Archetype: The Exhausted Craftsman

- **Age: 62** — Close to retirement, but pushed out just before the finish line.
- **Physical:** Wore the same corduroy blazer for a decade. Smelled faintly of stale coffee and printer toner. Had a habit of cleaning his glasses on his tie when a meeting was going south.

## Relationship with Devin

- **The Mentor:** He hired Devin in 2011. He was the one who said, "We don't need a framework for this, just write a clean Class." 
- **The Mutual Respect:** They communicated in short, cryptic sentences and shared a deep, unspoken hatred for "Product-Led Growth."
- **The Protection:** For years, Bob's primary job was "vague-ing" the Board. He would take their demands for "10x scaling" and "Agile transformations" and translate them into "Devin is working on the database," which bought Devin another six months of peace.

## The Fall: "10x or Nothing"

- **The Incident:** Six months ago, the Board brought in a consultant who mentioned "LLM-driven development" thirty-eight times in one hour. 
- **The Resistance:** Bob told them that "hallucinating code isn't an engineering strategy." 
- **The Exit:** He was "retired" on a Tuesday. No party. Just a cardboard box and a LinkedIn post from the CEO about "refreshing our leadership for the AI era."

## Legacy

- **The Vacuum:** His absence is why Greg is so loud. Greg isn't filling Bob's shoes; he's filling a void that Bob used to keep closed with sheer technical authority.
- **Devin's Trauma:** To Devin, Bob's departure was the day the "adults" left the building. Now, the children are playing with matches and calling it "Innovation."
